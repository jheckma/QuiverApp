Toric diagram  &  triangulation (fan) recomputing…

click a cyan dashed internal edge to flop the triangulation. click any lattice dot to add/remove a diagram point.
a blow-up does one of TWO different things — the two button rows above keep them separate:
• resolve the singularity (diagram fixed): each lattice point of the diagram is an exceptional divisor of the singular cone. ⊕ a dimmed point blows that divisor up; ⊖ blows it back down (red-tinted cells = residual orbifold singularities). All divisors down = the singular cone (e.g. C³/ZM×ZN: one big cell, legs with multiplicity); all up = the full crepant resolution (the default view).
• blow up a point of the base surface: adds a NEW exceptional P¹ that was not there before — the diagram GROWS (⊕ outside the diagram inserts the ray νi+νi+1; dP0 → dP1 → dP2  → dP3); ⊖ on a corner contracts a −1-curve (F0 = P¹×P¹ has none: minimal). Needs a cone over a surface (exactly one interior point) — C³ and the conifold have no surface point to blow up.
