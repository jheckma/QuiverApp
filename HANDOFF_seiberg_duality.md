## What was the blocker, and how it was solved
The urban-renewal/meson move produced the correct phase-II *quiver* but its new
edges had no embedding, so the **edge homology** (the Z² cochain feeding the
Kasteleyn Newton-polygon certificate) could not be read off geometrically — and
the naive composite rule certified 0 of 54 candidates.

**Fix = `solve_homology(dimer, target_hull)`** (`conformalmanifold/inverse.py`):
solves the cochain from the rotation system alone.
1. **Face-closure (cocycle):** every gauge face (φ-orbit of darts) closes —
   `Σ_d s_d·h(edge_d) = 0`. (Verified: the stored geometric homology of all 7
   seeds satisfies exactly these rows.)
2. **Gauge-fix** a spanning tree of the bipartite W/B graph to `h = 0` (kills
   the coboundary freedom).
3. The residual 2-dim space is `H¹(T²)=Z²` (the torus periods); take a
   **saturated** integer basis via `_integer_kernel` (integer column-echelon /
   unimodular column ops — saturated by construction, no sublattice trap, pure
   stdlib). With a saturated basis the **identity frame already certifies**; a
   small unimodular sweep remains only as a defensive fallback.

This recovers the stored homology for all 7 seeds and certifies the F0 phase-II
dual (6 certifying orderings, was 0).
